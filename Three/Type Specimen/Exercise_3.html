Introducing Helvetica, a widely used sans-serif typeface developed in 1957 by Swiss typeface designer Max Miedinger, with input from Eduard Hoffmann. Helvetica is a neo-grotesque or realist design, influenced by the famous 19th century typeface Akzidenz-Grotesk and other German and Swiss designs. Its use became a hallmark of the International Typographic Style that emerged from the work of Swiss designers in the 1950s and 60s, becoming one of the most popular typefaces of the 20th century.
